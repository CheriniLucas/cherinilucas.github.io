My first contact with AutoCAD was in the 90's with AutoCAD 14, I didn't understand how it worked at all, but I liked it and I was determined to learn how to use it. Already in high school, without news that they were going to teach me how to use it, I got a printed manual and the new version of the moment, AutoCAD 2000.

In a couple of months I went from not understanding anything to making complex drawings using only the console and I had even dabbled a bit in three-dimensional graphics.
